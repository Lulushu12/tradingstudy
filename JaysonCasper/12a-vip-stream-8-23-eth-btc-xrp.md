## Format of the session (repeated per asset: ETH, then BTC, then XRP/ADA)
For each coin, the instructor repeats the same top-down workflow taught in earlier lessons, live:
1. Clear the chart, re-mark **monthly → weekly → daily** S&R levels from scratch (scanning left for sticking points), color-coded.
2. Pull Fibonacci retracements from multiple recent swing lows to the same swing high (or vice versa in a downtrend) to find where retracement/extension levels **overlap each other** — treated as the strongest kind of confluence.
3. Overlay VPVR/Fixed Range volume profiles (old range vs. new "breakout" range) to find POC/VAH/VAL and high-volume nodes; check Naked Daily POCs (via a separate charting tool, ExoCharts) for extra confluence.
4. Collapse everything down into a small set of **"key levels"** (a dedicated chart layer) — only the zones with multiple confluence factors — rather than keeping every intermediate line visible.
5. From those key levels, define explicit **long zones** and **short zones** for the week, each with an entry trigger condition (bullish/bearish divergence on specific lower timeframes), a stop-loss location, and staged take-profit levels (TP1–TP4).

## Notable reiterated/refined concepts
- **Fib confluence via multiple swing points**: pulling several Fibonacci retracements from different valid swing lows to the same swing high, then looking for where their levels land on the same price — this is the same technique highlighted in the Fibonacci Part 3 livestream, applied repeatedly here.
- **"Global golden pocket"**: refers to the golden pocket (0.618–0.65 zone) of the *entire* current macro leg/move, as distinct from smaller local golden pockets of sub-legs — treated as the highest-priority resistance/support to watch for the week.
- Old range boundaries (value area high/low from a prior consolidation range) continue to act as support/resistance long after price has broken out of that range — reinforced with a live example (an old range's value-area-high level coinciding almost exactly with a fresh Fib/volume confluence zone).
- **Counter-trend scalping**: the instructor explicitly takes counter-trend scalps (shorting inside an uptrend) when a high-confluence resistance zone lines up with a bearish Market Cipher signal, while distinguishing this from swing-trade conviction — sizes and manages these differently (locks in profit early, smaller size) versus a "real" trend-following swing entry.
- **Weekly levels lose reliability the more they're tapped** (same rule as the daily/weekly lesson) — explicitly reiterated when reviewing which old weekly lines are still worth keeping.
- Live example of a missed scalp entry (a wick into the golden pocket with a 6-minute bullish divergence) used to illustrate how fast and specific these lower-timeframe entry windows are — reinforcing that entries are typically triggered on 1–12 minute charts even when the overall setup is planned from daily/weekly levels.
- "Chickens drinking water" is referenced again as the instructor's own name for what's more commonly called a **swing failure pattern (SFP)** — taking out a high/low with a wick before reversing — used here as a bullish setup example (wick below a low, quick reclaim) as well as a bearish one (wick above a high before dropping, mentioned as a possible scenario near resistance).
- Plans are explicitly dated/time-boxed: the instructor notes that Fibonacci-derived "key levels" from a given day's analysis need to be re-pulled if price moves meaningfully by the next day — the levels aren't meant to be treated as fixed forever.
