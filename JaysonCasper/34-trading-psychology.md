# Trading Psychology

Source lesson: trading-psychology
(Live session; personal anecdotes and open Q&A chat condensed to the substantive advice.)

## Core thesis
The goal of "trading psychology" is to remove emotion and make execution as mechanical as possible: do the analysis, define entries/exits/invalidation in advance, then follow the plan regardless of how the trade feels in the moment. Losses are an accepted, expected part of a properly risk-managed strategy — the emotional work is accepting that a good process can still produce a losing trade.

## Nine numbered tips given
1. **Do your TA and trust it.** Mark levels in advance (each morning/evening) and only get interested in a trade when price actually reaches a pre-identified level — never enter based on a spontaneous "feeling" about the chart.
2. **Decide what you want to see on Market Cipher *before* price gets there.** Define the confirmation conditions in advance (e.g. "if price hits this level AND Market Cipher shows X, I'll take the trade") rather than deciding reactively once price arrives.
3. **Have a plan and don't deviate.** Know and be able to state your specific reasons for a trade before entering. Once in, don't flip direction because the trade is temporarily going against you — repeatedly reversing position on emotion was cited as the instructor's own early-career mistake and a reliable way to lose money.
4. **Take an early partial to guarantee breakeven-or-better** if a trade looks like it may not reach its first full target. This converts "win or lose" into "win big or win small/breakeven," at the cost of lower average gains on individual trades — explicitly framed as a deliberate risk/reward tradeoff, not free money.
5. **Accept that losses happen**, including meaningful ones (cites a range of 1–5% of account on a well-managed, high-conviction trade). Do not revenge-trade after a loss — the recommended response is to step away from the charts entirely for a while rather than immediately seeking to "win it back."
6. **Don't micromanage swing trades.** Zoom out and judge continuation/invalidation using a clear, pre-defined rule (example given: staying in a swing position as long as price holds above the 21 EMA on the relevant timeframe, exiting only on a confirmed break, ignoring lower-timeframe noise in between).
7. **Create explicit personal trading rules — both TA rules and "life rules."** TA rules = specific confirmation requirements per strategy. Life rules = boundaries on *when/where* you trade (e.g. never trade from bed unless reacting to a pre-set alert with pre-decided criteria; don't try to do analysis while distracted in public). The point is to prevent low-quality, distracted decision-making.
8. **Avoid "paralysis of analysis."** Since a case can always be constructed for both directions, endless second-guessing leads to inaction while price moves without you. Once a trade meets your predefined criteria, execute and move on rather than continuing to relitigate the decision — a good, low-risk entry is still objectively good even if the broader trend later goes against you.
9. **Lock in profits early when possible** (ties back to tip 4) — once part of a position is secured in profit, the rest of the trade becomes far less stressful to hold through volatility.

## Supplementary points from Q&A
- **DCA-ing into a position**: fine to do (splitting an intended entry into several smaller fills as price approaches/tests a level) as long as you're confident the level will hold — riskier than a single entry if the level fails, since you've committed more capital before confirmation. The instructor's personal preference is to DCA only into an *already-open, already-profitable* position (redeploying banked profit on dips) rather than DCA-ing into a brand new, unconfirmed bottom.
- **News sensitivity**: treated as secondary to chart-based technical analysis, especially for BTC/ETH — the observation offered is that big "news-driven" moves (e.g. a well-known Elon Musk BTC tweet) often coincide with a setup that was already forming on Market Cipher beforehand, suggesting the news may just be a convenient narrative rather than the actual cause. Altcoins are treated as far more news/sentiment-driven than BTC/ETH.
- **Portfolio allocation across BTC/ETH/altcoins**: no fixed formula given — described as personal and market-condition-dependent (more altcoin activity in bull markets, more BTC/ETH focus in sideways/bear conditions); the instructor mentions keeping a portion of BTC holdings untouched as a long-term reserve.
- **Confluence hierarchy**: support/resistance (including Fibonacci) on higher timeframes is described as always the first thing checked, with Market Cipher B as the second and primary follow-up layer; other tools (Market Cipher A icons, the "Crown RSI+EMA" indicator mentioned) are treated as minor supplementary reassurance, not core decision drivers. The instructor explicitly contrasts this "basic" approach with more complex methods (Elliott wave, harmonics) used by other traders, noting that in his observation those more complex methods tend to arrive at similar conclusions to plain S&R + Fibonacci.
