# How to Plan and Enter Trades

Source lesson: how-to-plan-and-enter-trades
(A live-style walkthrough of trading a range that forms after a large pump/dump, tying together S&R, Fibonacci, CDW/SFP, and CVD-absorption concepts from earlier lessons into one continuous real-time thought process.)

## Core warning: don't FOMO into a big move
The instructor's central message: chasing a large pump/dump directly is usually a losing move. Instead, treat a big impulsive move as the *start* of a tradable range, not something to chase.

## Why a big move creates opportunity (not FOMO)
- Price action generally alternates between **ranging and breaking out/down** repeatedly. After a large impulsive move, a new range almost always forms, and that range's boundaries become the framework for multiple subsequent trades.
- Process: mark the **first high** and **first low** of the emerging range as soon as they're established (requires a few candles of confirmation, not just one wick — a single candle spike doesn't count as a real high/low).
- Once the range's high/low are marked, pull a **Fibonacci retracement from that high to that low**, and mark the **0.618–0.786 golden-pocket zone** — this becomes the primary area to watch for scalp shorts (if price pushes up into it) or scalp longs (if it dips into the low side equivalent).

## The two default trade types at range boundaries
1. **CDW / swing failure pattern** at the range high or low (see the dedicated Chickens Drinking Water lesson) — a wick beyond the level that fails to close past it, confirmed with the same divergence/money-flow checks used throughout the course.
2. **Golden-pocket rejection/bounce**, ideally reinforced by **CVD divergence/absorption** (see the CVD Absorption & Coinalyze lesson) — a reaction inside the 0.618–0.786 zone confirmed the same way.
- Both require the usual Market Cipher confirmation (divergence, money-flow direction) before entering — the level alone isn't sufficient.

## As the range keeps expanding, keep re-pulling levels
- Every time price makes a new high or low that extends the range, treat the **entire updated range** (old low to new high, etc.) as the current working range and re-pull the Fibonacci/volume-profile levels against it. The example walked through shows this happening repeatedly over a session — golden pocket short → CDW long at the low → new high forms → re-pull → next golden pocket short → next CDW long, and so on, accumulating multiple trades from one evolving range.
- Volume profile (fixed range) pulled across the whole developing range gives extra confluence levels (value area high/low, point of control) that often coincide with the Fibonacci golden pocket or a CDW level — worth checking each time the range is re-drawn.

## Explicit trade-by-trade example given (illustrative, not literal price targets)
A single session's range produced roughly 8 identifiable setups (shorts at golden pockets/highs, longs at CDWs/lows), with most confirmed by Market Cipher B divergence and money-flow direction on the 12-minute timeframe specifically called out as the instructor's primary confirmation timeframe for these setups. Outcome tallied as mostly wins with one clearly acknowledged loss (~0.4–0.5%), against multiple wins in the 1–3% range — used to illustrate that losses are a normal, small, and acceptable part of the process when risk is kept tight (stop just beyond the invalidating wick/level).

## Decision discipline: take-or-skip logic
- At each level, the actual entry decision comes down to whether Market Cipher confirmations (divergence + money-flow direction, checked down to at least the 12-minute and often lower) are present — if yes, take the trade; if no, wait.
- If price blows through a level without any bearish/bullish signs, **don't chase it** — wait for either the *next* level in the plan, or for a retest of the level just broken, before entering. Missing an entry is explicitly framed as fine and preferable to a low-confidence entry: "it's better to miss a trade than to take one you're not confident in."
- No obligation to trade every day or every setup — patience for genuinely confirmed setups is presented as the actual edge, not the number of trades taken.

## Standing takeaway
This lesson is a demonstration/synthesis lesson rather than new theory — it shows the instructor's live mental process for planning a session around an evolving range: mark range extremes → pull Fibonacci + volume levels → watch for CDW or golden-pocket setups at each boundary → require Market Cipher confirmation before entering → keep updating the range as new highs/lows print.
