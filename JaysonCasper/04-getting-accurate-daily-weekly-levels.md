# Getting Accurate Daily & Weekly Levels

Source lesson: getting-accurate-daily-weekly-levels
(Supplemental follow-up to the timeframes/S&R lesson, addressing student questions about which levels are worth keeping on the chart.)

## Rule for creating a new daily/weekly level
- Scan left-to-right; a new level is confirmed when **at least two candles in a row** (same timeframe) fail to close/open beyond a specific price — i.e. two-plus candle bodies repeatedly rejected at the same point.
- Apply the same two-candle-body rule on the weekly timeframe for weekly levels (a weekly level isn't "official" until that weekly candle actually closes there).

## Why some levels are shown and others aren't
- **Untouched levels are the most reactive.** A level that has never been revisited since it was formed tends to produce the strongest bounce/rejection when price returns to it.
- **Each retest weakens a level.** The first touch after formation often gives a strong reaction; by the 4th-5th touch the level may get blown through entirely. Reaction strength decays with each touch — it doesn't guarantee a break, but confidence in it should drop.
- Practical filtering rule: keep untouched or lightly-touched levels on the chart at full weight. For a heavily-touched level, only keep/trust it if there's **confluence** — i.e. multiple independent signals lining up at the same price (a Fibonacci level, a high-volume node, etc., topics covered later in the course). Without confluence, a much-tapped level can be moved to a secondary "old levels" reference list rather than cluttering the main chart.
- Also distinguish "held as resistance/support cleanly" vs. "price pushed through and only retested it in passing" — the latter suggests a weaker future reaction from that level.

## Takeaway workflow
1. Look left, find 2+ candle-body rejections at a price → mark as daily/weekly level.
2. Judge importance by: (a) how many times it's been retested (fewer = stronger), and (b) whether other confluence factors line up there.
3. Keep the chart decluttered — drop low-confidence/no-confluence, heavily-touched levels rather than keeping every historical line.
