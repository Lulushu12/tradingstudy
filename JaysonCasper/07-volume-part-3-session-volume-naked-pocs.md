# Volume Part 3 — Session Volume & Naked POCs

Source lesson: volume-part-3-session-volume-naked-pocs

## Recap
- Visible Range VPVR → HVNs/LVNs, VAH/POC/VAL for whatever's on screen.
- Fixed Range → same VAH/POC/VAL concept but for a manually selected sub-range (e.g. from the start of the current trend/impulse leg to now). Above POC generally acts as support, below POC generally acts as resistance; VAH/VAL mark the edges of "fair value" — moves outside the value area tend to get pulled back in.

## New tool: Session Volume HD → Naked Point of Control (NPOC)
- Indicator: search **"Session Volume HD"** in TradingView, star it.
- Unlike Fixed/Visible Range, this plots a **separate volume profile for every individual candle** (per session) — each candle gets its own POC/VAH/VAL.
- On the daily timeframe, this gives a **point of control for every past day**.
- **Naked Point of Control (NPOC / NDPOC = naked daily POC)**: a daily POC that price has never revisited since it printed. These are unusually reactive levels — first touch after a long time untouched.
- Once price does trade back through an NPOC, it becomes "clothed" (instructor's own term) and loses most of its predictive power.
- Related, lower-value reference levels: **PDPOC** (previous day POC — only notable if untouched/naked), **PDVAH** / **PDVAL** (previous day value area high/low) — instructor doesn't weight these heavily unless they coincide with something else.

## Why NPOCs matter
- Two real trade examples cited: a short taken directly off a naked daily POC rejection (~115% gain held at time of recording), and a long taken where price dropped into a naked daily POC that also lined up with a Fibonacci level, triggering an immediate strong bounce (~11%+ quickly).
- Workflow: scan Session Volume HD for POCs never revisited → mark as "naked (daily) point of control" → check for confluence with other tools (daily/weekly hand-drawn levels, other volume levels, Fibonacci retracements). Confluence between an NPOC and a Fib level (e.g. a 0.786 retracement in the example) makes it a high-conviction zone.

## Standing methodology
Keep layering confluence: hand-drawn D/W levels + VPVR HVNs + Fixed Range POC/VAH/VAL + Naked POCs + (next lesson) Fibonacci — the more of these line up at one price, the higher-conviction the zone for a reaction.
