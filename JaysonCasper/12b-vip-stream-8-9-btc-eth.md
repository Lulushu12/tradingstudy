## Structure (same as other livestreams)
Per asset: clear chart → re-mark weekly/daily S&R (noting which have gone stale from repeated touches) → analyze volume across three separate ranges (an old multi-month range, the most recent uptrend leg, and a distinct high-volatility "Elon Musk tweet range" specific to this period of BTC/ETH history) → pull Fibonacci retracements/extensions → collapse everything into a small set of key-confluence zones → define explicit long/short setups with entry trigger, stop, and staged TPs.

## Reinforced/extended concepts
- **"Global" value area high/point of control**: when multiple separately-drawn volume ranges (old range, uptrend-only range, breakout range) all produce a POC or VAH landing at nearly the same price, that's treated as unusually strong confluence — explicitly called out when it happens (e.g. BTC's uptrend-leg POC lining up almost exactly with the old range's value-area-high).
- **Old range boundaries keep acting as S&R after breakout**: reiterated with a live example — the value area high of a prior multi-month range becomes the new floor once price trades above it; losing that floor again would be a bearish signal that price is "back in the range."
- **Stop-loss and position sizing rule stated explicitly**: never risk more than ~1% of capital per trade; determine position size backward from where the stop must go (above/below the last swing high/low) — e.g. an 0.8% stop-loss distance implies a specific max leveraged size to keep risk at 1%.
- **Profit-locking mechanic explained in detail**: on entering a scalp/short, take a small partial (e.g. 10%) fairly quickly and move the stop to breakeven — the logic given is that the realized partial profit covers the fee/slippage of the eventual stop-out, so the trade becomes "can't lose" once that's done; continue taking further small chunks (as little as 5%) on the way to target rather than waiting for one exit.
- **"Chickens drinking water" clarified further**: works on any timeframe and in either direction — the defining feature is a *fast, sharp* wick/spike beyond a level (up or down) that quickly reverses, sweeping liquidity before the real move; instructor says he specifically likes to see this pattern on the 1-minute chart as confirmation even when the broader setup is planned on higher timeframes.
- **Divergence + money flow reliability caveat**: a bearish/bullish divergence is less trustworthy if money flow is still moving in the *original* trend's direction (e.g. a bearish price/momentum divergence while money flow is still rising) — such divergences "don't play out as much," though they can still cause a partial reaction; full conviction wants both the divergence AND money flow turning together.
- Never treat a support/resistance "level" as an exact price — always a region/zone, and the instructor says he personally waits to see price actually touch/react at a zone rather than pre-placing orders far in advance.

## Concrete example setups discussed (illustrative, not literal future price targets)
- BTC: identified multiple resistance clusters going up (an "old range" ceiling ~48k, a further cluster ~51k combining the global golden pocket + weekly + a Fib 0.786, and a top cluster ~57–60k combining a 0.786 Fib + a daily level + the Elon-tweet-range POC) each proposed as a scalp-style short with a "chickens drinking water" (SFP) trigger, tight stop above the swing high, and staged TPs down to successive support/POC levels. A long setup was also defined much lower, at a confluence of the old range's value-area-high and the whole uptrend's point of control, contingent on a deep retracement with bullish divergence confirmation.
- ETH: similar layered resistance clusters identified (~33.5k confluence with a golden pocket, ~37.9k confluence of a weekly + POC + 0.786) as short setups; long setups were left more open/reactive ("need to watch price action develop") since ETH had fewer clean historical reference points at the time, illustrating that this method sometimes yields a clear plan and sometimes requires waiting for more structure to form before committing to specific long levels.
