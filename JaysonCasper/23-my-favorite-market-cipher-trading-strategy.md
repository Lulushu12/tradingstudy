# My Favorite Market Cipher Trading Strategy

Source lesson: my-favorite-market-cipher-trading-strategy
(A YouTube video embedded in the course; walks through several of the instructor's real trades to demonstrate his everyday go-to strategy, simpler than the full 6-timeframe framework in Market Cipher 103.)

## Philosophy: simplicity over complexity
Explicit stance against over-complicating charts (harmonics, Elliott wave counts, etc.) — the instructor's claim is that fewer variables means fewer chances to freeze up or make mistakes ("paralysis of analysis"). His personal approach is deliberately reduced to: hand-drawn S&R + EMAs + Fibonacci + Market Cipher.

## The step-by-step process (this lesson's version, simpler than the 103 series)
1. **Mark key levels** — daily and 4H S&R, drawn by hand as in earlier lessons. No trade is considered unless price reaches one of these.
2. **Have a plan before price arrives** — decide in advance ("if price reaches this level, I'll look for a long/short") rather than reacting in the moment. Set a price alert at the level and wait.
3. **Check Market Cipher B on the 4-hour** once price reaches the level:
   - Explicit rule: **don't enter a long right as a red dot is actively printing** at a support — wait until some candles have passed since that red dot (giving momentum "time to cool off") before treating a bounce as valid. Mirror logic applies to shorts at resistance with green dots.
   - Once enough time has passed since the opposing-color dot, start watching for the momentum waves to visibly curve toward a bottom/top (the light/dark blue crossing), even before a confirming dot has printed.
4. **Drill into lower timeframes** (6-minute or even 1-minute for fast-moving entries) looking for: a clear divergence (price and momentum moving opposite directions), a clear directional read (consecutively higher lows for a long / lower highs for a short), and a money-flow color crossover — entering once these line up, typically right as or just after the money-flow cross confirms.
5. **Take staged profits and move stop to breakeven** as price moves favorably — repeatedly emphasized ("nobody ever went broke taking profit") as essential to converting a good read into a locked-in win rather than risking giving it back.

## Worked examples (illustrative, not literal future levels)
- Multiple long entries off a daily support that had already flipped from resistance to support — each following the pattern: 4H shows momentum starting to curve up (pre-dot) after a red dot had time to age, then 6-min/1-min shows a clean bullish divergence + money-flow cross → entry, staged take-profits hit.
- A long entered off a bounce specifically at the **confluence of a daily support and the 21 EMA (Market Cipher A ribbon 5) on the 4-hour** — flagged as a particularly reliable combination: "if we're in an uptrend and the 21 EMA is white, a touch of that EMA has a good chance of a significant bounce."
- A short entered off a rejection at a resistance level right before what many viewers expected to be a breakout to a new all-time high — instructor's reasoning for fading the "obvious" breakout was the same process (lower-timeframe money-flow cross + clear bearish direction right at the rejection), illustrating that the same method works against crowd sentiment, not just with it.
- Explicit trade-invalidation logic reiterated: because entries are taken at a defined level with a stop below/above the last swing low/high, if price breaks that invalidation point, the loss is capped (example given: <1% stop-loss) and the trader simply looks for the next setup rather than holding through invalidation.

## Standing takeaway
This is presented as the instructor's default, lower-effort version of the Market Cipher 103 framework — same core logic (level → higher-timeframe cooldown/momentum check → lower-timeframe divergence + money-flow confirmation → staged profit-taking) but condensed to fewer timeframes and less formal bookkeeping, intended for traders who find the full 6-timeframe process too heavy for everyday use.
