# *Bonus* MORE Powerful Trading Strategies

## Strategy 1: The "4-24" (4-hour / 24-minute) Strategy
- **Only for strongly trending markets** (clear sustained uptrend or downtrend with impulsive legs and shallow pullbacks) — explicitly not meant for sideways/choppy conditions.
- Setup: split the screen into two charts — 4-hour and 24-minute of the same asset.
- Wait for the 4-hour Market Cipher B to print a **trigger wave** (a green dot higher than the prior dot, in the direction of the trend) — trigger wave defined loosely as "any dot that's higher than the wave to its left" following an anchor low.
- Once that trigger wave starts printing on the 4H (no need to wait for it to fully confirm), switch to the 24-minute chart and enter on **whichever comes first**: (a) money flow crossing the zero line in the trade's direction, or (b) the first same-direction dot printing on the 24-minute.
- **Directional filter**: in an uptrend, only use this to go long — only take a short via this method if the 4H is also showing a bearish divergence (price higher high, momentum lower high) at the same time. Mirror rule for downtrends (only short, unless a bullish divergence appears). This filter is called out as important — using the strategy against the prevailing trend without a divergence is discouraged.
- Multiple historical BTC examples walked through (both uptrend and the ~May crash downtrend) showing this producing a string of high-win-rate trades, including a couple of scalps and one flagged explicitly as a likely loss/breakeven among the winners — presented as generally high win-rate but not undefeated.
- **Why 4H + 24-min specifically**: explained as a "divide by 10" relationship — 4H = 240 minutes, 240/10 = 24. The instructor states this ratio produces two timeframes that read well together, and implies the same ratio could be used to derive other complementary timeframe pairs (though only 4H/24-min is demonstrated in depth here).

## Strategy 2: The "Hidden/Regular" Strategy (for catching pumps early, inside an established trend)
- Goal: catch the bottom of a pullback *within* an existing uptrend (or top of a pullback within a downtrend) earlier than the 4-24 strategy would, to capture more of the subsequent move.
- Setup: split screen into **1-hour** and **6-minute** charts (the instructor notes 6-minute is derived the same "divide by 10" way as before, roughly).
- Step 1 — on the 1-hour: look for a **hidden bullish divergence** (momentum prints a lower low while price prints a higher low, inside an uptrend) — a continuation signal that pullback is ending. More reliable if the momentum lows lining up are both large ("anchor-sized") waves, though not required.
- Step 2 — on the 6-minute (or 3-minute if needed for more resolution): look for **either**:
  - (a) a **regular bullish divergence** (price making a lower low while momentum makes a higher low) forming right as/after the 1-hour hidden divergence appears, or
  - (b) absent a clean regular divergence, simply a clear anchor → trigger wave sequence with a money-flow crossover in the trade's direction.
- Entry: typically on the money-flow crossover on the lower timeframe, once one of the above lower-timeframe confirmations appears — explicitly *not* on the 1-hour's green dot alone (backtested example showed entering purely on the 1-hour dot led to a quick stop-out; waiting for the 6-minute confirmation captured a much larger portion of the move — cited as capturing several percentage points more profit than the 4-24 method would have on the same move).
- Explicitly caveated: these hidden-divergence pullback setups don't occur constantly — described as situational, not a constant signal generator like the 4-24 strategy.

## Standing distinction between the two strategies
- 4-24 = simpler, mechanical, works best riding an already-established strong trend, lower analytical overhead.
- Hidden/Regular = more complex multi-condition read, but aimed specifically at catching the *start* of a trend-continuation leg earlier (better entry price) than the 4-24 method would.
