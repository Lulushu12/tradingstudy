# Divergences

Source lesson: divergences

## Four things that can diverge (not just momentum waves)
Reiterates that a "divergence" can be checked independently across **momentum waves, money flow, VWAP, and RSI** — the more of these four show the same divergence simultaneously, the stronger the setup. A real trade example (a short taken right at a top) is cited where all four diverged together against a daily resistance level, described as an especially high-quality signal because of that stacking.

## Regular divergence (reversal signal) — definition recap
- **Bearish**: price makes a higher high; the oscillator (momentum/money flow/VWAP/RSI) makes a lower high at the same time → expect a top/reversal down.
- **Bullish**: price makes a lower low; the oscillator makes a higher low → expect a bottom/reversal up.
- Draw the comparison by connecting the same two reference points (e.g. two dots/highs) on both price and the oscillator.

## Hidden divergence (continuation signal) — definition recap, with a physical analogy
- **Hidden bullish**: in an uptrend, price makes a *higher* low while the oscillator makes a *lower* low → trend expected to continue upward.
- **Hidden bearish**: in a downtrend, price makes a *lower* low while the oscillator makes a *higher* low → trend expected to continue downward. (Framed in the lesson as building extra downside "energy.")
- Physical analogy given for hidden bullish: like dropping a ball from progressively higher points onto a trampoline — the deeper "drop" in the oscillator relative to price's shallower low is read as pent-up energy for a bigger continuation move.
- Practical note: hidden divergences tend to resolve more slowly/gradually than regular divergences, especially on lower timeframes — they're described as an early "harbinger" rather than an immediate trigger, whereas regular divergences often produce a sharper, more immediate move.
- The instructor states he sometimes acts on a hidden divergence while it's still forming, without waiting for full confirmation, when other context (already in an established trend) supports it.

## Standing takeaway
Divergences work best layered on top of hand-drawn support/resistance levels (as in the Market Cipher 103 lessons) rather than traded in isolation — a divergence forming exactly at a pre-marked level is presented as one of the highest-conviction entry patterns in the whole course, including entries at what turn out to be exact tops/bottoms.
