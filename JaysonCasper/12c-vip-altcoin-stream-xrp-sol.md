## Same core workflow, applied to altcoins (XRP, then SOL from scratch)
- Full top-down pass: monthly → weekly → daily S&R (noting which are "tapped" vs. "untapped" — see below), then bull-market-range volume profile + a more local fixed-range volume profile + naked daily POCs (via ExoCharts, since this platform's own naked-POC tool doesn't cover every altcoin), then Fibonacci retracements/extensions from multiple swing points, then collapse into key-confluence long/short zones.
- Notable: for SOL, the instructor explicitly says he'd never analyzed this asset before and demonstrates building the entire level structure live from a blank chart — useful as a template for approaching any new asset cold.

## "Tapped" vs. "untapped" levels — terminology clarified
- **Untapped** = price has not returned to a level since it was first formed (maximum reactivity expected).
- **Tapped** = price has already come back and interacted with the level at least once (reactivity decreases with each additional tap) — consistent with the daily/weekly-levels lesson's rule, just given explicit terminology here. The instructor marks tapped/older levels into a separate "other/old" layer rather than deleting them, since they can still matter if the level ever wins back confluence with other tools.

## The 4H/24-minute ("4-24") strategy, explained in more concrete mechanical detail
- Simple momentum approach: enter long when Market Cipher B prints a green dot with rising money flow on both the 4-hour and 24-minute charts; exit/close when a red dot prints.
- Refinement given here: after an initial red-dot exit, it's fine to **re-enter on the next green dot as long as money flow is still higher than it was at the previous entry** — i.e. keep riding a series of green/red dot cycles as long as each successive money-flow peak is higher than the last. Once money flow's peaks start declining, stop re-entering and treat that as the sign to stay out (or exit if still in).
- Framed explicitly as a simpler, lower-effort ("lazy") alternative to full confluence analysis — described as reliably profitable on its own even without layering in S&R/Fibonacci/volume.

## Position sizing / leverage note (elaborated)
- When a resistance/support "zone" is unusually wide (little historical price action to narrow it down, as with newer/less-charted assets like SOL at the time), the instructor's approach is to use **lower leverage precisely because the invalidation zone is wide** — sizing leverage down so that even an imprecise entry keeps the liquidation price safely beyond the whole zone, rather than waiting for a single precise level.
- General rule repeated: pick position size such that liquidation price stays beyond the trade's invalidation zone, and accept that wider zones require proportionally lower leverage/contracts.

## Confluence patterns highlighted in this session
- Multiple examples of **two different Fibonacci pulls landing their retracement levels on the exact same price** (e.g. two separate 0.618 levels from different swing points coinciding) — repeatedly called out as especially strong confluence, consistent with the technique shown in the Fibonacci Part 3 livestream.
- A recurring "golden pocket + naked daily POC + weekly level" triple-stack was found and flagged as a key support on XRP.
- "Chickens drinking water" (SFP) is used again both as a bullish setup (wick below a support that's quickly reclaimed) and explicitly as a bearish one for a large low-confidence resistance "box" (a strong pump through the top of the box while momentum diverges bearishly = the swing-failure short signal, described in detail: watch for divergence to appear progressively across timeframes from higher down to lower, and the ideal entry is the final wick/divergence on a very low timeframe like 6-minute).
