# Volume Part 2 — Fixed Range

Source lesson: volume-part-2-fixed-range
(Reinforces Volume Part 1, then adds the Fixed Range tool.)

## Recap: Visible Range VPVR
- Same VAH / POC / VAL concepts as Part 1: value area high, point of control, value area low from the volume-by-price profile.
- These levels flip polarity like normal S/R: e.g. a VAL that was resistance can flip to support, then back to resistance once lost — price often continues to respect the old range boundaries even after breaking out of the range.
- Workflow reinforced: draw weekly → daily → (optionally 4H) S/R by hand first, then overlay VPVR, mark high-volume nodes (HVNs) as extra S/R lines, and specifically look for **confluence** where an HVN lines up with an already-marked weekly/daily level — that's a stronger level than either alone.
- Peaks in the profile = HVNs (best used as S/R), valleys = LVNs (weaker, price moves through quickly).

## New tool: Fixed Range
- Unlike Visible Range (which profiles whatever is currently zoomed into view), **Fixed Range** lets you manually select a specific start/end (click-drag from one candle to another) and get a volume profile (its own VAH/POC/VAL) for just that sub-range.
- Typical use case: after price breaks a major weekly/range level, drag a Fixed Range from the exact candle where that level broke up to the present. This isolates "what's the new volume structure since we left the old range" and often produces a POC/VAH/VAL that lines up closely with levels found other ways — reinforcing confluence.
- Example given: after losing a multi-month weekly support/range, a Fixed Range drawn from the breakdown candle produced a POC that matched an HVN already found via Visible Range, and a VAL that pinpointed a bounce to the dollar.

## Layered level-marking process (as taught progressively)
1. Weekly S/R by eye (sticking points).
2. Daily S/R by eye.
3. (Optional) 4H/lower S/R by eye.
4. Visible Range VPVR → mark HVNs, especially where they overlap steps 1–3.
5. Fixed Range on specific sub-ranges of interest (e.g. since a major breakout/breakdown) → another POC/VAH/VAL layer.
6. Don't keep every line visible at once — declutter, keep only the confluence zones that matter.
7. Once levels are marked, **Market Cipher** (covered in later lessons) is used to judge whether price will actually bounce/reject at a level or blow through it — level-marking alone doesn't predict which outcome occurs.
